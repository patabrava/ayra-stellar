## Design Context

### Users
Stewards, grantee contacts, and admin operators using an operational transparency portal. They are trying to approve applications, submit or verify the first Stellar payout address, publish updates, and move batches through settlement without guessing what happens next.

### Brand Personality
Precise, calm, and accountable. The tone should feel operational rather than promotional: clear enough for first-time users, strict enough for admin workflows, and trustworthy around payment and privacy boundaries.

### Aesthetic Direction
Editorial utilitarian. Light surfaces, restrained emphasis, explicit state labels, and short guidance that points to the next step. Avoid generic SaaS filler, opaque banners, and “replacement-only” language when the user is actually in a first-time setup step.

### Design Principles
- Every state message should say what happened and what happens next.
- First-time setup must be visually and verbally distinct from later replacement flows.
- Route-local guidance should stay specific to the steward or admin job in front of the user.
- Payment states should make verification and locking explicit before batches are created.
- Keep the surface calm and low-noise, but never at the expense of clarity.
